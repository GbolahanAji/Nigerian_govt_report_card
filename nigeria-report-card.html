The grades

Each administration is graded by how often its average beat the 20-year benchmark across all measurable indicators. A is a strong majority of wins; D means most indicators ran worse than the long-run norm.

Sources & methodology

How to read this

This is an independent compilation, not an official government product. Figures are drawn from the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS), Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN), the World Bank, UNODC and the Armed Conflict Location & Event Data Project (ACLED). Where an indicator is surveyed only periodically (poverty, for example), intervening years are interpolated or estimated and labelled as such.

Indicator notes

Minimum wage in USD is derived by dividing the statutory naira minimum wage by each year's average exchange rate. Unemployment figures from 2022 onward use the NBS/ILO labour-force methodology adopted in 2023 and are not directly comparable with earlier years. Homicide rate (per 100k) follows the UNODC definition, which excludes deaths in armed conflict — captured separately under "conflict deaths." Conflict-death figures are approximate, anchored to ACLED political-violence reporting; the 2014–2022 period is well documented, while 2005–2010 values are lower-confidence reconstructions of the pre-peak insurgency era and 2025 is a partial-year estimate. This dashboard is intended for analysis and public discussion; verify against primary sources before citing.
